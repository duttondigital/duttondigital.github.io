Google Forms is free. Facebook Pages are free. Instagram business accounts are free. There's no such thing as free.

When a product costs nothing, you're not the customer. Your data is.

## The trade you didn't agree to

Every time a customer fills out your Google Form, Google learns something. Every interaction on your Facebook Page feeds Meta's advertising machine. Every booking through a free platform gives that platform data they can monetise.

You're not just using a free tool. You're handing over your customer relationships to a company whose entire business model is selling attention and information.

## What they're actually getting

It's not just the obvious stuff — names, emails, phone numbers. It's patterns. When do your customers book? What do they buy together? How do they respond to price changes? What makes them come back?

This data is extraordinarily valuable. Valuable enough that companies will give you sophisticated software for free just to collect it.

And once they have it, you have no control over how it's used. On ad-supported platforms like Facebook and Instagram, your customers might see ads from your competitors based on their interaction with your page. Their information gets combined with data from dozens of other sources to build profiles they never consented to.

## The dependency trap

Free platforms are also sticky by design. The more you use them, the harder it becomes to leave.

Your customer list lives in their system. Your booking history is in their database. Your reviews are on their platform. Moving away means starting from scratch — or paying for expensive data migration that may not even be possible.

This isn't accidental. It's the business model.

## What's the alternative?

Pay for things. Seriously.

When you pay for software, the incentives align. The company makes money by serving you well, not by extracting value from your customers.

Yes, it costs more upfront. But the hidden costs of "free" — loss of customer data control, dependency on platforms that don't have your interests at heart, contributing to a surveillance economy — are far higher.

## Questions worth asking

Before you sign up for that free tool, ask:

- How does this company make money?
- What data are they collecting?
- Who else can access it?
- What happens to my data if I leave?
- What happens to my data if they get acquired?

If you can't answer these questions, you're not making an informed choice. You're just hoping for the best.
