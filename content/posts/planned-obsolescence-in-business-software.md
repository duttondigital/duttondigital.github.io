Your accounting software worked fine yesterday. Today there's an update. Now it needs a subscription. And half the features you relied on are "premium."

Welcome to modern business software.

## The subscription creep

Software used to be something you bought. You paid once, you owned it.

Now everything is a subscription. Prices go up. Features get moved behind higher tiers. Things that used to be included become add-ons. The product you signed up for slowly transforms into something more expensive and less useful.

You're locked in, so you pay.

## Forced upgrades

Even when you're happy with what you have, you don't get to keep it.

Software companies routinely end support for older versions. Sometimes they shut them down entirely. Your workflow, your data, your muscle memory — all irrelevant. Upgrade or lose access.

## Complexity as a feature

Simple tools don't justify high prices. So tools get complicated.

Features get added whether anyone asked for them or not. Interfaces get redesigned. New dashboards, new "AI-powered" additions. The software becomes bloated, slower, harder to use.

Meanwhile, you just wanted to send invoices.

## What actually matters

Most businesses need simple things done reliably: track money, keep customer information organised, manage appointments, communicate clearly.

You don't need AI features. You don't need a dozen integrations. You need tools that work, stay stable, and don't hold your data hostage.

## Finding alternatives

Look for:

- **Small vendors** — Companies not chasing growth at all costs tend to offer more stable products.
- **Simple over sophisticated** — The tool that does less but does it reliably beats the tool that does everything poorly.
- **Transparent pricing** — If the price is clear and stays clear, that's a good sign.
- **Easy data export** — If you can take your data with you, you're not trapped.

The goal is software that serves you, not software that extracts maximum revenue from you.
