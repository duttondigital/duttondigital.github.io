Your accounting software worked fine yesterday. Today there's an update. Now it needs a subscription. And half the features you relied on are "premium."

Welcome to modern business software.

## The subscription creep

Software used to be something you bought. You paid once, you owned it, you used it until it stopped meeting your needs.

Now everything is a subscription. Which sounds reasonable — ongoing development costs money, servers cost money, support costs money.

But watch what happens over time. Prices go up. Features get moved behind higher tiers. Things that used to be included become add-ons. The product you signed up for slowly transforms into something more expensive and less useful.

You're locked in, so you pay.

## Forced upgrades

Even when you're happy with what you have, you don't get to keep it.

Software companies routinely end support for older versions. Sometimes they shut them down entirely — especially cloud-based tools. Your workflow, your data, your muscle memory — all irrelevant. Upgrade or lose access.

This isn't about security patches or critical fixes. It's about forcing you onto the new version so they can sell you the new pricing.

## Complexity as a feature

Simple tools don't justify high prices. So tools get complicated.

Features get added whether anyone asked for them or not. Interfaces get redesigned. New integrations, new dashboards, new "AI-powered" additions. The software becomes bloated, slower, harder to use.

But it looks like progress. It justifies the subscription. It gives the sales team something to talk about.

Meanwhile, you just wanted to send invoices.

## The integration trap

Modern software loves integrations. Connect everything! Sync your data across platforms! It sounds powerful.

It's also a trap. The more integrated your tools, the harder it is to leave any of them. Your data flows through a web of dependencies. Switching one tool means untangling connections to a dozen others.

This isn't accidental. Vendors know that integration means lock-in. They actively encourage it.

## What actually matters

Most businesses need simple things done reliably:

- Track money in and out
- Keep customer information organised
- Manage appointments or orders
- Communicate clearly

You don't need AI features. You don't need a dozen integrations. You don't need a platform that does everything badly instead of one thing well.

You need tools that work, stay stable, and don't hold your data hostage.

## Finding alternatives

They exist. Look for:

- **Small vendors** — Companies that aren't chasing growth at all costs tend to offer more stable, focused products.
- **Simple over sophisticated** — The tool that does less but does it reliably beats the tool that does everything poorly.
- **Transparent pricing** — If the price is clear and stays clear, that's a good sign.
- **Easy data export** — If you can take your data with you, you're not trapped.

The goal is software that serves you, not software that extracts maximum revenue from you.
